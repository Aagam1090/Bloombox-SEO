    Details About the Event

    BloomBox, the E-cell of KJSCE organised Ennovate’20, a techno-business competition blending together both Entrepreneurship and Innovation with the aim ‘Make your work know it’s Worth!’.     Panel Session

    Details About the Event

    The panel session titled 'Entrepreneurship, Engineering and Innovation- the pillars of a successful start-up' was conducted by BloomBox on 21st January 2020. The reputed panelists were the alumni of different branches from our own college.

    The panelists answered a variety of questions that our young entrepreneurs had in their minds and also shared stories of their invaluable experiences. They answered critical questions like how to come up with a breakthrough idea for their start-up, whether it was necessary to have a mentor to guide you along the tough path of building one's own company, if they should have a co-founder for better growth and support, where to get the required financial resources and how to overcome the daunting fear of failure. Our panelists came up with relatable and witty answers, which did not only answer the questions thoroughly but also captured the attention of our young motivated student entrepreneurs.

    Date of Event :- 21st January 2020

    Ennovate 2019

    Previous Next

    Details About the Event

    Ennovate is an absolute blend of Entrepreneurship, Innovation and Technology, where students from every background can come together to innovate and mould their ideas to realistic solutions and progressive business plans.

    Ennovate’19 is a Techno-Business event with a tagline ‘Make your work know its worth’. Participants are asked to build a prototype of website/ web application demonstrating the use of any technology and provide innovative solutions for real-world problems through their prototype. Along with the prototype, the teams are also asked to provide a business plan for their prototype.

    Gaurang Shetty has been part of the startup ecosystem through RiiDL for some time now and he said that the students were fortunate enough to be in a campus where they have an incubator inside the campus so they can spend some time and learn, shadow there and see how their lives are like.

    Statisitcs
    1. Prize Money: 1st place – Rs. 7000 + Goodies.
    2nd place – Rs. 5000
    3rd place – Rs. 1500 + Goodies.
    4th place – Rs.1500 + Goodies.
    2. Number of Participants: Total 18 teams participated in the competition from colleges all over Mumbai with major participation from K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering.
    3. Duration: The participants were given a time of 15-20 days to build their prototype. The event was held on 5th April 2019 in K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering.
    4. Specifics:
    Round 1 (Elimination Round) : 5th April 2019
    The competition started with the elimination round. Each team were given 7 minutes (5 mins for presenting and 2 mins for Q&A) for Round 1 where they had to pitch their ideas through their prototype.
    Round 2 (Final Round) : 5th April 2019
    The top 8 selected teams were then given 12 minutes per team (7 mins for presentation and 5 mins for Q&A) for presentation. In this round, participants presented their problem statements, their respective solutions to it using technology and their business models.
    5. Judges: Our highly recognized panel of judges for this event were -
    1. Mr. Rohit Kilam (CIO/ CTO, Aditya Birla Finance Ltd.)
    2. Mr. Neil Savant (Founder & CEO, NUOS Home Automation)
    3. Mr. Raj Dhiravani (Incubation Manager, riidl)
    4. Mr. Mehul Ved (Founder, Stratahive)
    5. Ms. Avani Sakhapara (Assistant Professor, Department of Information Technology, KJSCE)
    6. Ms. Sunayana Jadhav (Assistant Professor, Department of Information Technology, KJSCE)

    Details About the Event

    B-Plan Workshop

    Previous Next

    Details About the Event

    BloomBox, E-Cell KJSCE in collaboration with E-Cell, SIMSR organized a B-plan workshop by the RedBull Basement University

    The workshop was conducted by -
    1. Mr. Romil Shah (A Business Analyst and a Headstarter)
    2. Mr. Abhishek Barari (Head of Operations and Finance, MycuteOffice)

    Mr. Romil Shah gave an overview of three waves of Entrepreneurs. The interactive discussion facilitated by Mr. Abhishek Barari included the concept of Lean Canvas which was demonstrated using a real life example which covered the problem statement, solutions to it, it’s feasibility, the customer segments correlating to the problem statement directly, key metrics, various channels to publicize, revenue systems, unique value propositions (UVP) and unfair advantages to the idea.
